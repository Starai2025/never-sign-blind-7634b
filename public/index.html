Top 3 Hidden Traps
Critical Risk Retainage Loophole

Clause 14.3 states retainage is only released upon "Owner's final acceptance" rather than "Subcontractor's completion of work." This could delay your final payment by 24+ months.

⚡ VERDICT: High financial exposure for small-cap subs.
Operational Warning 48h Notice Period

Section 8.2 requires written notice of delay within 48 hours. Standard practice is 5–7 days. Failure to meet this window waives your right to any time extensions.

⚡ VERDICT: Administrative burden; high probability of waiver.
Legal Review Required Unilateral Risk Shift

Article 21 forces you to indemnify the Owner even for the Owner's own negligence. Unenforceable in some states but dangerous.

⚡ VERDICT: Aggressive indemnification; potential insurance gap.
Pushback Language — Retainage
"Retainage shall be released within 30 days of Subcontractor's substantial completion of its specific scope, notwithstanding project-wide completion."
