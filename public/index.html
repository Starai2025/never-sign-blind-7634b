Most Bad Subcontracts Don't Look Dangerous at First

Small subcontractors don't usually lose money because they're bad at the work.

They lose money because they signed a deal that trapped them — and by the time they realized it, the job was underway and the leverage was gone.

One clause. Wrong language. Signed under time pressure. That's how a $400,000 paving job turns into a cash flow problem.

⚠
Payment delayed longer than expected
Vague payment terms push cash collection weeks — or months — past what you planned.
⚠
Retainage stuck for months past your finish date
Retainage tied to full project closeout means your money sits while other subs finish up.
⚠
One clause kills change order recovery
A 48-hour notice window in field conditions is basically impossible to hit. Extra work goes unpaid.
⚠
Good job, bad deal — because of the language
Broad backcharge rights and vague liability shifts mean you absorb risk that belongs to the GC.
