CashTrap Scan™ — Money Traps Found (5)
HIGH
§12.3 — Pay-When-Paid Clause
What it means: Your payment is contingent on the GC getting paid by the owner. No deadline, no recourse if the owner is late or disputes a payment.
Pushback: Request a pay-when-paid backstop — 45-day outside payment deadline regardless of owner payment status.
HIGH
§7.1 — Retainage at Project Closeout
What it means: Your 10% retainage isn't released when your paving scope is done — it's held until full project closeout. On a $380K job, that's $38,000 held 60–90+ days after your last day on site.
Pushback: Request retainage release tied to your substantial completion, not overall project closeout.
MED
§18.4 — 48-Hour Written Notice for Extra Work
What it means: Any extra work requires written notice within 48 hours or you waive recovery rights. In field conditions, this window is realistically impossible to hit consistently.
Pushback: Request 7 calendar days for notice, or minimum 5 business days. Remove "time is of the essence" language.
MED
§9.2 — Backcharges Without Prior Notice
What it means: GC may deduct backcharges from your payments with no advance warning or documentation requirement.
Pushback: Require 5-day written notice with documentation for all backcharges. Deductions without notice are waived.
LOW
§22.1 — Liquidated Damages Not Capped to Your Scope
What it means: Delay exposure not scoped to your actual work — you could be assessed LD charges for delays partially outside your control.
Pushback: Cap LD exposure at your contract value, limited to delays directly caused by your scope.
